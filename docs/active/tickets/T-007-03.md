## Context

Currently sky, terrain, and road all share the same projection — stars, clouds, terrain silhouettes, and road objects move at the same apparent rate relative to camera Z advancement. Real depth perception comes from parallax: distant objects move slowly, near objects rush past. This ticket introduces parallax layering so the scene reads as having genuine depth.

## Implementation

### 1. Define depth layers

| Layer | Content | Parallax factor | Scroll rate |
|-------|---------|-----------------|-------------|
| Sky | Stars, gradient | 0.0 | Stationary (only rotates with pitch) |
| Far background | Sun/moon, nebula glow | 0.05 | Near-stationary |
| Mid background | Cloud silhouettes | 0.15 | Gentle drift |
| Near background | Terrain island silhouettes | 0.4 | Moderate scroll |
| Road surface | Grid, road markings | 1.0 | Full speed |
| Foreground | Sprites, player car | 1.0 | Full speed |

Parallax factor = fraction of camera Z movement applied to that layer's scroll.

### 2. Modify sky.rs star rendering

Stars currently use fixed screen positions that only streak with speed. Instead:
- Store star positions in world-space polar coordinates from a "sky sphere"
- Apply camera pitch to shift star field vertically
- Stars should NOT scroll with camera_z (parallax factor 0.0) — they're infinitely far
- Speed streaks remain as-is (visual effect, not positional)

### 3. Modify terrain.rs parallax

Terrain silhouettes (islands, clouds) currently scroll at full road speed. Split into two sub-layers:

**Clouds** (mid-background, parallax 0.15):
- Scroll at 15% of camera Z delta
- Positioned above horizon, overlapping slightly
- Gives clouds a "painted on the sky dome" feel

**Terrain islands** (near-background, parallax 0.4):
- Scroll at 40% of camera Z delta
- Positioned at/below horizon
- Creates the sense of distant landmasses vs near road

### 4. Add parallax offset to terrain projection

```rust
fn terrain_z(world_z: f32, camera_z: f32, parallax: f32) -> f32 {
    world_z - camera_z * parallax
}
```

Use this adjusted Z when computing screen position for each terrain layer.

### 5. Camera pitch affects layer offset

When `camera.pitch > 0` (looking up, e.g., at a hill crest):
- Sky layers shift down on screen (see more sky)
- Road layers shift up (road compressed)
- Each layer shifts proportionally to `(1.0 - parallax_factor) * pitch`

This ties naturally into T-007-02's hill system: cresting a hill pitches the camera up, revealing more sky.

### 6. Preserve render order

Parallax doesn't change the painter's algorithm order:
1. Sky (farthest)
2. Clouds
3. Terrain silhouettes
4. Road + grid
5. Sprites
6. Effects overlay

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Stars remain stationary as camera advances (no Z-scroll)
- [ ] Clouds drift slowly relative to road speed
- [ ] Terrain silhouettes scroll at ~40% road speed
- [ ] Parallax difference is visually obvious when comparing layers
- [ ] Camera pitch (from hills) shifts layers appropriately
- [ ] No visual gaps between layers (seamless horizon transition)
- [ ] Sun/moon remains nearly fixed on screen (far background layer)
- [ ] Performance: parallax adds no measurable overhead (same number of draw calls)
