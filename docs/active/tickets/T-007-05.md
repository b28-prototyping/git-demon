## Context

After the structural changes (unified projection, segments, parallax, camera dynamics), this ticket handles integration tuning and visual polish. The individual systems need to be calibrated together so the overall experience feels cohesive and the sense of speed is maximized.

## Implementation

### 1. Calibrate near-plane and draw distance

With 1/z projection, the near-plane constant determines how aggressively near geometry is stretched. Tune `NEAR_PLANE` so that:
- At idle speed, the road grid looks similar density to current (no jarring change)
- At max speed, the grid near the camera stretches dramatically (speed sensation)
- Draw distance produces at least 3-4 visible hill crests at max zoom

### 2. Tune hill parameters

Iterate on hill sine wave frequencies and amplitudes:
- Idle: gentle rolling, peaks barely visible — don't distract from the screensaver calm
- Mid speed: clear undulation, occasional crest that hides the road ahead
- Max speed: dramatic crests and dips, brief moments of "airtime" where the road drops away

Avoid hills so steep they hide all forward sprites — the player should always see at least one segment of road ahead.

### 3. Harmonize speed lines with 1/z projection

Speed lines currently use linear length scaling. With 1/z projection, lines should be denser near the vanishing point and sparser near the screen edges. Adjust:
- Line spawn radius to be uniform in projected space, not screen space
- Line length to scale with `1/z` at the line's radial position

### 4. Motion blur depth-awareness

Currently motion blur is uniform alpha across the screen. With perspective, near objects move faster in screen space than far objects. Apply depth-weighted motion blur:
- Near the camera (bottom of screen): stronger blur
- Near the horizon: less blur
- Simple gradient: `blur_alpha = base_alpha * (1.0 + (y - horizon_y) / (screen_h - horizon_y) * 0.5)`

### 5. Grid line density at distance

With 1/z projection, horizontal grid lines will naturally compress toward the horizon. Verify this looks good — if lines become sub-pixel, cull them rather than rendering aliased noise. Consider a LOD threshold: stop drawing horizontal grid lines past the Z where their screen-space spacing drops below 2px.

### 6. Sprite pop-in softening

With hills, sprites may suddenly appear as they crest a hill. Add a brief fade-in (alpha ramp over 3-4 frames) when a sprite first becomes visible to avoid hard pop-in.

### 7. Screenshot comparison and regression testing

Capture reference screenshots at key speed tiers (idle, mid, max, VelocityDemon) before and after the full perspective overhaul. Verify:
- The neon grid aesthetic is preserved
- Color palette and bloom are unchanged
- HUD is unaffected
- Performance budget is met

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Near-plane calibrated: idle looks familiar, max speed stretches dramatically
- [ ] Hills are dramatic at speed but never hide all forward visibility
- [ ] Speed lines integrate with 1/z projection (dense at center, sparse at edge)
- [ ] Motion blur is stronger at screen bottom than horizon
- [ ] Grid lines don't alias at far distances (LOD cull below 2px spacing)
- [ ] Sprite hill-crest pop-in is smoothed with fade
- [ ] Overall aesthetic is preserved (neon cyber grid, bloom, color palette)
- [ ] Frame time budget: full render pass ≤ 8ms at 200×60 terminal (120fps headroom)
